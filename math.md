Previously, as a mathematics student, I specialized in representation theory. During my Bachelors and Masters degrees,
I studied certain invariant of bilinear forms arising from representations of simple Lie algebras called
the Dynkin index. My PhD research topic lies in the intersection of algebra, representation theory, algebraic geometry 
and mathematical physics. More specifically, I explored shifted Yangians, they are certain noncommutative versions of 
Coulomb branches, which are spaces appearing in the context of mathematical physics.
